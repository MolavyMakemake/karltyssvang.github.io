Karl Olav Tyssvang

I am a postgraduate student at the Mathematical Institute, University of Oxford. I will commence my PhD this October as part of the ERC starting project GeoFEM (Geometric Finite Elements) with Prof. Kaibo Hu as my primary advisor, and Prof. Snorre H. Christiansen as my secondary advisor.

My academic background is in differential geometry and functional analysis, which is complemented by an extensive experience with programing. I am curious about how we can represent and discretize geometries and PDE (partial differential equations) on computers, and of how the two relate. For example, if you have a PDE on a sphere should you discretize the sphere before discretizing the PDE? We can approach such questions using tools from the fields of discrete differential geometry and finite elements methods.

I obtained my MSc in applied mathematics at the University of Oslo in 2025. My thesis Non-Euclidean Elements was on structure preserving discretizations of PDE in the hyperbolic spaces. In this context the philosophy of virtual element methods was an important tool for dealing with the underlying geometry in an intrinsic manner. I believe the computational restrictions which arrise in dealing with an underlying metric can inspire further development of established element methods.
